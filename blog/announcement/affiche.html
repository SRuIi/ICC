Tutorial for MyEtherWallet send-receive interconnected cash ICC token

2017-12-26

1.Open the official website（ https://www.myetherwallet.com/）

2.The wallet can also be downloaded and opened by browser for use： https://myetherwallet.github.io/ ... wallet-locally.html

3.Google adds extended applications and install tutorials

Create a wallet

Enter a strong password to create a wallet，be sure to keep the password well

Then download the keystore file,make sure to store the file

Go on to the next step

Properly keep your private key (a string of characters)

At this point, the wallet is created, and click the figure "save your address

You should have 3 things at this time: the Keystore file, the password, the private key (a string of characters)

Note: please keep these three things properly, or you will not find it back just as you lose a wallet in real life!

Unlock (open) the wallet

There are 2 ways to unlock the wallet:

One is through the Keystore file + password recovery

The other is to unlock it through a private key (without a password)

Below is a rough appearance of the wallet interface:

Each wallet has an address, which can simultaneously send and receive the tokens of ETH and ERC20 protocols (ICC included)

The lower right is the display of token asset:

It is not fully displayed, and you can open and view through "Show Tokens", and then conceal it through the "Hide Tokens"

Receive tokens

Your account address is your ETH/ICC receipt address

Send

The sending requires a poundage of at least 0.01ETH, so you have to make sure you have a 0.01ETH balance in your account.

In the column of “sending the ether currency / sending token”, fill in the target address and the amount of transfer and choose the token type. If you have no special requirement on the Gas limit, then follow the system default, and then "generate the transaction" -- "send the transaction" -- "confirm", and finally send ICC tokens.
